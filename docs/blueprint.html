Why this way — scored against the brief

Clinical importance ✓
Judging criterion #1. Admin burden is the measured, top-ranked pain (19 h/wk, 9,093 FTE) — the metric quantifies it.
Technical feasibility ✓
Judging criterion #2. FHIR-native, standard stack, runs on a laptop — deployable in 6–12 months.
Safety & privacy ✓
Judging criterion #3. Local processing, synthetic only, AI restates source-backed facts and never invents clinical content. (Judge El Emam.)
Workflow fit ✓
Judging criterion #4. No new login, fewer clicks, converts to tasks, integrates via FHIR. (Judge Radhakrishnan.)
Actionable outputs
Design requirement: don't just generate text — draft the form, queue the task. That gap is where care falls through.
Calibrated trust
Design requirement: confidence + evidence + edit/override. Human-in-the-loop; the COMPASS brief says augment judgement, don't bypass it.
Practical affordability
Design requirement: small clinics, tight margins. Lean local stack — no expensive infrastructure or per-seat licensing.
One shared FHIR foundation
One substrate for both branches → tomorrow's fork is cheap, today's risk is retired.
